Evaluate the user's game mechanic for design quality. Read `references/framework.md` for the full evaluation model and `references/gotchas.md` for common analysis mistakes to avoid.

The core reframe: a mechanic is not a feature -- it is a verb. The player does something, and the quality of that doing determines whether the game has a foundation. Everything built on top of a weak verb (progression, narrative, polish) is decoration on a cracked slab.

Evaluate across these dimensions:

- **Isolation quality** -- is the core verb satisfying with nothing else around it?
- **Depth profile** -- low floor, high ceiling, discoverable techniques in between?
- **MDA coherence** -- do the mechanics produce dynamics that produce the intended emotional experience?
- **Interaction quality** -- do mechanics combine to create emergent situations, or do they operate independently?
- **Transplant integrity** -- if borrowed, does the mechanic carry hidden dependencies or aesthetics from its origin?
- **Degenerate strategy risk** -- does optimal play conflict with the intended experience?

When a mechanic is borrowed from another game or genre, pay special attention to what was left behind. The mechanic worked because of systems that weren't copied.

Be direct and specific. End with the 1-2 highest-leverage changes. Judge the mechanic on the designer's terms -- not every mechanic needs every dimension.
