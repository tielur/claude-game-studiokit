Evaluate the user's game design or mechanic for its feedback loop structure. Read the framework in `references/framework.md` for the full evaluation model, and check `references/gotchas.md` for common analysis mistakes to avoid.

The core idea: feedback loops are either positive (amplifying -- success breeds success, failure breeds failure) or negative (dampening -- success is slowed, failure is cushioned). Most games need both, and the interesting design question is how they interact, when they fire, and whether players can read them.

For each loop in the design, assess what it amplifies or dampens, whether it creates the intended pacing and tension, and whether it sends clear or contradictory signals to the player. Be direct and specific. End with the 1-2 highest-leverage changes.

Judge the design on its own terms. Not every game needs the same relationship with feedback loops.
