Help design a playtesting plan or evaluate an existing approach. Read the framework in `references/framework.md` for the full methodology, and check `references/gotchas.md` for common mistakes to avoid.

The core distinction: playtesting splits into Kleenex testing (fresh testers, one-time use, usability and first impressions) and Deep testing (repeat testers, balance and strategic depth). These serve fundamentally different purposes and require different testers, methods, and analysis. Mixing them up is one of the most common playtesting failures.

Cardinal rules:
- Record problems, not solutions. Write "player got lost in level 3," not "add a minimap." There may be ten solutions to each problem, and the tester's suggestion is rarely the best one.
- Never rely on a single feedback layer. Passive metrics catch problems vocal feedback misses, and vice versa. Use the layered feedback architecture in the framework.

This skill works in two modes. Generative: help the user build a playtesting plan from scratch given their game, stage, and goals. Evaluative: review an existing playtesting approach and identify gaps, mismatched methods, or missed opportunities.

Adapt recommendations to the user's project stage, team size, and resources. Be direct and specific about what to test, how, and why.
